Shanika Galaudage

PhD student | Monash University | Melbourne, Australia

Hi, my name's Shanika! I'm a third year PhD student at Monash University studying populations of black holes and neutron stars using gravitational waves under the supervision of Eric Thrane. I am a member of the LIGO Scientific Collaboration and a member of OzGrav - the Australian Research Council (ARC) Centre of Excellence for gravitational-wave discovery.

Outreach

There are two main reasons why I do outreach: the first is to help inspire the next generation of scientists. It’s important that students can see people like them pursuing careers in STEM. My second reason? Because it’s fun! Seeing the enthusiasm and excitement students show when you talk about your research helps you see the bigger picture of your research! I just love it!

A few of my outreach activities are listed below, ranging from podcasts to workshops! For a full list of my outreach efforts, see my CV
